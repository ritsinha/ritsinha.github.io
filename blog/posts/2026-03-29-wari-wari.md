I don't know anything about music. I can't read it, I can't play it, and I have no training in any tradition. I've just listened across many genres over the years, and developed preferences along the way. What I find interesting about *Wari Wari* is the thread it pulls across different eras and styles — the same composition, handled very differently each time. I thought it was worth sharing.

Raag Yaman is one of the most commonly performed raagas in Indian classical music. It belongs to the Kalyan thaat, is typically performed in the early evening, and is considered a good entry point for listeners new to the form — it's melodically accessible without being simple. In Bengali, the raag is called Raag Emon. Unlike many Sanskrit-origin words that shift the *ya* sound to *ja* in Bengali, this one didn't. I found that an interesting piece of information.

## Ustad Azmat Hussain Khan — Agra Gharana

[youtube: J0_uXZFYMeg]

This is the classical rendition. Ustad Azmat Hussain Khan is from the Agra gharana, one of the older lineages in Hindustani vocal music. Notice the dhrut taal — the fast-cycle rhythmic section in the latter half.

---

## Kaushiki Chakraborty

[youtube: ZF41ywVAFOU]

Kaushiki is one of the stronger modern voices in Indian classical music. This is a full rendition, and it holds up well.

---

## Berklee College of Music

[youtube: l7_036v84TE]

Two performers. One is singing; the other is beatboxing the entire instrumental ensemble — tabla, sarangi, tanpura drone — with his voice. It runs a little slower than the others, which actually makes the structure easier to follow if you're less familiar with the form.

---

## Coke Studio Bangladesh

[youtube: 1_Hfi1EV0-k]

A Bengali adaptation, produced with a full orchestra and a fair amount of stylistic layering. Different melodic choices, different feel. More production. Worth hearing as a contrast to the others.

I have 72 peer-reviewed publications and 67 patents. That is not a small number. And yet, for almost everything I find genuinely interesting — a half-formed idea, an observation about how causal inference is being misapplied in industry, a paper I read at 11pm that changed how I think about something — there has been no good place to put it.

Papers take months. A LinkedIn post feels performative. A tweet is too short to say anything careful.

This blog is the middle path.

## What I plan to write here

Mostly technical. Probably around:

- **Causal inference in practice** — the gap between what identification assumptions say and what practitioners actually do.
- **AI agents and evaluation** — I've been thinking a lot about how to evaluate systems that involve chains of LLM calls. Standard benchmarks miss almost everything that matters.
- **Experimentation** — online A/B testing at scale, where the statistics gets genuinely hard.
- **Occasional opinion** — about how research is done, what gets published, and what doesn't.

I'm not trying to build an audience. I'm trying to think more carefully by writing things down. If something is useful to you, that's a bonus.

## A note on frequency

I will not commit to a posting schedule. I have two kids, a dog named Nash, and a full-time research job. Posts will appear when they appear.

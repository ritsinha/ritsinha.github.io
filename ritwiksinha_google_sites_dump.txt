# Research

I am an Applied Statistician and Data Scientist. As a Statistician, I have had good fortune of working on data from many different domains. I have peer reviewed publications in Genetics, Clinical Trials, Medicine, Marketing, Computer Science and Geology.

# Family
I come from a Bangla speaking family from the eastern Indian city of Kolkata or Calcutta.

I am married to Moumita Sinha and we have two sons, Nishaant and Aneesh. We also have a dog, his name is Nash. We currently live in Cupertino, California.

My father Shri Santanu Sinha Chaudhuri (and his blog) worked in a Bank for about twenty five years and part of his responsibilities was to move from one city to another whenever we got too comfortable in one place. As a result, I spent my schooling years in Hyderabad, Visakhapatnam, Trivandrum, Bangalore and Calcutta. I accept though, that seeing so many places and meeting so many people was wonderful experience most of the time.
Presently my father is engaged in writing in English and translating Bengali works of fiction into English.

My mother Smt. Arundhati Sinha is a teacher by profession and has the experience of having taught in more than ten schools. Presently she has an adorable pre-school Ankur Khelaghar (ankurkhelaghar.googlepages.com) in Parnasree, Calcutta.

My sister Sohini Sinha, also lives and works in Bangalore, India.

Chorki or Chakradhar was our dear dog who passed away a few years back.

# Chorki
Chorki was a six week old male pup when he came to our house and after many years of trials and tribulations on his and my families part he has managed to become the most important individual in the family. Chorki is a Smooth Fox Terrier. The "Smooth" stands for a smooth coat, not smooth like James Bond.

He was born on the 5th of February 1995 and came to our house on the 18th of March the same year. Chorki is a wonderfully naughty dog, and it is impossible not to love him. And he will fetch anything that you throw. He will chase anything that moves, like lizards or crows and even flowing water. He does not like being photographed, so I do not have many good pictures of him. Here are some of the nicer ones (unfortunately in low resolution). Chorki passed away in 2010.
